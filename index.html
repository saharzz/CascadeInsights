Sahar Zangeneh, PhD

Researcher | Educator | Consultant

I am a statistician with expertise in design and analysis of experimental and observational studies, including individual- and community-randomized clinical trials, complex sample surveys, and observational cohort studies; and generating real-world evidence using diverse data sources. My methodological background focuses on statistical methods for addressing missingness and non-random selection, including situations where missingness or selection depends on unobserved factors (missing not at random).

I have wide-ranging experience in building and leading teams, and conducting cross-cutting collaborations across a broad range of biomedical and epidemiological domains spanning chronic and infectious diseases, neonatology, sexual and mental health, and nutrition; data sources ranging from clinical and survey data to electronic health records; and populations including children and adolescents, gender minorities, and racial and ethnic minorities.

I am an educator and communicator who uses data to tell stories of people and communities. Sometimes the data speaks for itself, and sometimes we need sophisticated methods and external data to address nuances and get a better picture. I am a skeptic by training, but adaptable, unafraid of new paths and always eager to learn new ideas.
